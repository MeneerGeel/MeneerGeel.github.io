1. Tinkering, trial-and-error and brainstorming

Tinkering, trial-and-error and brainstorming are compared here according to two different sources; for the dictionary definition the Cambridge Dictionary was used, and as I try to avoid using generative AI I asked a friend for their definitions of the three terms.

Cambridge Dictionary (C)

Generative Friend (G)

Comments

Tinker - to make small changes to something, especially in an attempt to repair or improve it.

Taking something and messing with it, trying to make it do something different than it currently does. This could be repairing it, but it could also mean giving it a different function than originally intended.

I like G's approach of putting repairing and repurposing into the same category of "changing the current functionality"'. Otherwise, these definitions match quite well, and from both you can conclude that of the three terms, 'Tinkering' is the only one that, by definition, starts with an existing artefact.

Trial and error - a way of achieving an aim or solving a problem by trying a number of different methods and learning from the mistakes that you make.

It is really in the name, try and fail at different approaches until one works.

A noticable difference is that C emphasises learning from mistakes that were made, whereas G sees the 'trials' as seperate events that are executed until one appears to work. In my interpretation, trial-and-error is at least partly implicit in both tinkering and brainstorming.

Brainstorming - an activity or business method in which a group of people meet to suggest a lot of new ideas for possible development.

Really a group process where you try to explore something or have a clear problem to solve. It is a group activity because building off of each other's ideas is integral to the idea.

I think G makes a good point in putting emphasis on building off of each other's ideas, which C omits. I would also say this is a key part of the definition, otherwise it would be a room of individuals writing down their thoughts instead of a group brainstorming. This makes this the only term of the three which is by definition group-based.
